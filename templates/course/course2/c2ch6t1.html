Structures and union

In the C programming language, structures and unions are two powerful and flexible mechanisms for organizing and manipulating data. They allow programmers to create custom data types that can store different types of data under a single name, making it easier to manage and access related pieces of information. Here's an introduction to structures and unions in C:

Structures:

Definition: A structure is a composite data type that groups together variables of different data types under a single name. Each variable within a structure is referred to as a member or field.

Uses:

  • Structures are commonly used to represent complex entities with multiple attributes, such as a person with a name, age, and height.
  • They help in organizing related pieces of data into a single unit.
