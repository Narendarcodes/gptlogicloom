jumps in loops

In C programming, "Jumps in loops" refer to keywords that alter the normal flow of control within a program. These statements allow you to transfer control from one part of your code to another. There are three primary jump statements in C: break, continue, and goto.

Flow chart

Syntax for break:

break;

Explanation:

break Statement in C:

  • Purpose: The break statement is used to exit or terminate a loop (such as for, while, or do-while) or a switch statement prematurely.
  • When encountered inside a loop or switch statement, the break statement immediately exits the loop or switch, transferring control to the statement immediately following the loop or switch.
