Arithmetic Operations on Characters

  • In C, we can perform arithmetic operations on characters because each character is represented by its ASCII value.
  • For example, if you have a character 'A', its ASCII value is 65.
  • Here's a simple example:

    • Program.c

    Output:
    Result: 1

  • In this example, subtracting the ASCII values of 'A' and 'B' will give you the result of 1.
  • You can perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division on characters based on their ASCII values.
  • Keep in mind that the result will be an integer.
